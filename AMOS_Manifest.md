# AMOS Manifesto
## The Autonomous Mission Operating System

Autonomous systems are transforming robotics, defense, logistics, and infrastructure.

Drones, robots, vehicles, and sensors are becoming more capable every year.

But one major problem remains:

**These systems do not work well together.**

Each platform has its own software stack, control interface, and operational logic.

As a result, autonomous systems remain fragmented.

---

# The Missing Layer

Robotics frameworks like:

- ROS
- PX4
- ArduPilot

provide low-level control for individual machines.

But they do not solve the problem of **coordinating missions across many autonomous systems simultaneously**.

The world needs a new layer.

A layer that can:

- orchestrate missions
- coordinate autonomous teams
- integrate sensors and platforms
- maintain human authority
- operate across domains

---

# The Mission Operating System

AMOS is an attempt to build that layer.

AMOS is not a drone controller.

It is not a robotics framework.

AMOS is a **mission operating system**.

It provides the control plane for:

- multi-domain robotic forces
- autonomous mission orchestration
- human-machine teaming
- event-driven operations
- distributed autonomous coordination

---

# Platform, Not Product

AMOS is designed as a **platform ecosystem**.

It allows developers to build:

- asset adapters
- mission packs
- autonomy algorithms
- analytics modules
- integrations

The goal is to create an environment where autonomous systems can work together seamlessly.

---

# Human Authority Matters

Autonomy should assist human operators, not replace them.

AMOS is designed around the principle of **human-on-the-loop supervision**.

Humans define intent.

Autonomous systems execute tasks.

---

# A Shared Foundation

AMOS aims to become a shared foundation for autonomous mission systems.

A place where researchers, engineers, and organizations can collaborate on the next generation of autonomous operations.

---

# The Vision

Imagine a world where:

- drones coordinate with ground robots
- maritime vessels patrol autonomously
- sensors trigger coordinated responses
- missions adapt dynamically to changing conditions

All supervised by humans.

All coordinated by a single mission operating system.

That is the vision behind AMOS.
